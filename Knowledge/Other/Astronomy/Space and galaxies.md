🌌 The “path” from the universe to you
## 1. The Universe

Everything that exists—space, time, matter, energy. It began about 13.8 billion years ago with the Big Bang.

---

## 2. Our galaxy: the Milky Way

A massive spiral galaxy containing 100–400 billion stars.

Shape: spiral with curved arms
Size: ~100,000 light-years across
At the center: a supermassive black hole called Sagittarius A*

---

## 3. Our location inside the Milky Way

We’re not in the center—we’re in a quieter suburb.

Arm: Orion Arm (a smaller spiral arm)
Distance from center: ~27,000 light-years

This is actually a pretty safe place—far from the intense radiation near the galaxy’s core.

---

## 4. Our star system: the Solar System

Centered around our star, the Sun.

8 planets (Mercury → Neptune)
Asteroids, comets, dwarf planets
The Sun contains 99.8% of all the mass in the Solar System

---

## 5. Our planet: Earth 🌍

Third planet from the Sun
Only known place with life (so far)
Has liquid water, atmosphere, and stable temperatures
🧠 Most important things to know about the Milky Way

---

## 6. It’s moving

The Milky Way isn’t still—it’s traveling through space and rotating.

Our Solar System orbits the galaxy center
One full orbit = ~225 million years (a “galactic year”)

---

## 2. It’s part of a group

We’re not alone as a galaxy.

Member of the Local Group
Includes the Andromeda Galaxy

👉 Fun fact: Andromeda is on a collision course with the Milky Way in ~4 billion years (don’t worry—stars are so far apart that collisions are unlikely).

---

## 3. It’s mostly invisible

Most of the Milky Way’s mass is dark matter—we can’t see it, but we know it’s there because of gravity.

---

## 4. Stars are constantly being born and dying

New stars form in gas clouds
Old stars explode as supernovae
These explosions create elements like iron, gold, and oxygen

👉 Meaning: the atoms in your body were made in stars.

---

## ✨ Cool facts

🌠 When you see the Milky Way in the night sky, you’re looking through the dense disk of our own galaxy
🚀 There may be billions of Earth-like planets in the Milky Way
⚡ The galaxy is surrounded by a huge invisible halo of dark matter
🕳 The black hole at the center isn’t “eating everything”—it’s actually pretty quiet right now
🧭 We’ve never seen the Milky Way from the outside (because we’re inside it!)
🧭 Simple mental map

Think of it like this:

Universe → Milky Way → Orion Arm → Solar System → Earth → You
