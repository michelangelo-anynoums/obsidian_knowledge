# 3. Electricity Fundamentals (CORE)

## 3.1 Electric Charge

- Electrons as charge carriers
- Positive vs negative charge
- Charge conservation

## 3.2 Voltage

- Electrical potential difference
- “Pressure” pushing electrons
- Measured in volts

## 3.3 Current

- Flow of electric charge
- Measured in amperes
- Electron flow in conductors

## 3.4 Resistance

- Opposition to current flow
- Material properties affecting resistance
- Heat generation in resistors

## 3.5 Ohm’s Law

- Relationship between voltage, current, resistance
- Linear electrical behavior in simple circuits

## 3.6 Electrical Power

- Power = voltage × current
- Why devices heat up
- Energy efficiency in systems

---

# 4. Circuits (FUNDAMENTAL)

## 4.1 Series Circuits

- Same current through all components
- Voltage divides across components
- Equivalent resistance increases

## 4.2 Parallel Circuits

- Same voltage across branches
- Current divides
- Equivalent resistance decreases

## 4.3 Capacitors

- Store electric charge
- Charge/discharge behavior
- Used in memory and filtering

## 4.4 Inductors

- Store energy in magnetic field
- Oppose changes in current
- Used in power regulation

## 4.5 Diodes

- One-way current flow
- Rectification
- Protection circuits

## 4.6 Transistors (MOST IMPORTANT)

- Electronic switch
- Basis of logic gates
- Builds all modern CPUs

---

# 5. Electromagnetism (NETWORK FOUNDATION)

## 5.1 Electric Fields

- Fields created by charges
- Force without contact
- Field direction and strength

## 5.2 Magnetic Fields

- Produced by moving charges (current)
- Interaction with electric fields
- Basis of motors and storage

## 5.3 Electromagnetic Induction

- Changing magnetic field creates current
- Transformers and power systems
- Wireless energy transfer

## 5.4 Electromagnetic Waves

- Combined electric and magnetic oscillations
- Travel through space
- Basis of all wireless communication
