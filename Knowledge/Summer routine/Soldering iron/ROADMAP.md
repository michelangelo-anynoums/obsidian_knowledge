# 🧰 Soldering Iron Roadmap

## 1. Fundamentals of Soldering

### What soldering is

- Joining two metal surfaces using a filler metal (solder)
- Electrical + mechanical connection (not just “gluing wires”)

### Key concepts

- **Wetting**: solder flows and bonds to metal properly
- **Heat transfer**: heat must go into the joint, not the solder alone
- **Clean surfaces**: oxidation prevents good joints
- **Capillary action**: molten solder flows into gaps

---

## 2. Tools & Equipment

### Essential tools

- Soldering iron (preferably temperature-controlled)
- Solder wire (lead or lead-free)
- Flux (critical for clean joints)
- Soldering stand + sponge or brass wool
- Wire cutters / flush cutters
- Tweezers

### Important notes

- **Temperature control**
    - ~320–350°C (lead solder)
    - ~350–400°C (lead-free solder)
- **Iron tip types**
    - Conical → precision work
    - Chisel → general electronics (recommended starter)

---

## 3. Solder Types & Materials

### Solder wire

- Leaded (easier, smoother flow)
- Lead-free (safer, harder to work with)

### Flux

- Removes oxidation during heating
- Types:
    - Rosin (common electronics)
    - No-clean (less residue)
    - Water-soluble (needs cleaning after)

### PCB basics

- Pads, traces, vias
- Through-hole vs SMD components

---

## 4. Basic Soldering Technique

### Step-by-step process

- Heat the joint (not the solder first)
- Apply solder to the heated joint
- Let it flow naturally
- Remove solder, then iron
- Let it cool without movement

### Good solder joint characteristics

- Shiny (for leaded solder)
- Smooth and cone-shaped
- Fully covers pad and wire

### Bad solder joints

- Cold joint (dull, grainy)
- Excess solder (blob)
- Insufficient solder (weak connection)

---

## 5. Desoldering & Repair

### Tools

- Desoldering pump
- Solder wick (braid)
- Flux for cleanup

### Techniques

- Heat + pump for through-hole parts
- Wick for cleaning pads
- Reflow technique for fixing cold joints

---

## 6. Safety Guidelines

### Critical safety rules

- Always use a stand (never leave iron loose)
- Work in ventilated area (fumes matter)
- Don’t touch tip (obvious but important)
- Unplug after use

### Health considerations

- Avoid inhaling flux fumes directly
- Wash hands after handling lead solder

---

## 7. Common Mistakes (and fixes)

- **Solder not sticking**
    - Dirty surface → clean + add flux
- **Blobby joints**
    - Too much solder → reduce feed
- **Cold joints**
    - Not enough heat → increase contact time
- **Burnt PCB pads**
    - Too much heat → lower temp or faster technique
