**Thermal paste** (also called TIM – Thermal Interface Material) is a key part of any PC build because it fills microscopic gaps between the CPU and the cooler, allowing heat to transfer efficiently. Even a good CPU can run noticeably hotter if the paste is poor or incorrectly applied.

## 🛠️ How to apply thermal paste (correct method)

1. **Clean the CPU and cooler base**
    
    - Use isopropyl alcohol (90%+) and a lint-free cloth.
        
    - Remove all old paste completely.
        
2. **Apply a small amount**
    
    - The most common method:  
        👉 a **pea-sized dot in the center** of the CPU
        
    - Do NOT spread it manually in most modern CPUs.
        
3. **Mount the cooler immediately**
    
    - Press it straight down evenly.
        
    - Screw it in cross-pattern (diagonal tightening) for even pressure.
        
4. **Do not overapply**
    
    - Too much paste = worse thermal performance and possible mess.

## ⚠️ Important things to know

- More expensive ≠ always much better (differences are often small)
    
- Paste usually lasts **3–6 years** (sometimes longer depending on heat cycles)
    
- Avoid “liquid metal” pastes unless you are experienced (they are electrically conductive and risky)
    
- Reapplying is usually only needed if:
    
    - You remove the cooler
        
    - Temperatures rise unusually over time
