## 🧠 CPU Naming & Generations (Intel vs AMD Ryzen)

---

# 🔵 Intel CPUs

## 1. Naming Structure (Modern Intel)

Example: **Intel Core i7-14700K**

- **Core i7** → Product tier (performance level)
    
    - i3 → Entry-level
        
    - i5 → Mid-range
        
    - i7 → High-end
        
    - i9 → Enthusiast / flagship
        
- **14** → Generation (14th Gen = newest mainstream as of ~2024–2025)
    
- **700** → SKU number (higher = better within same tier)
    
- **Suffix (K, F, etc.)**:
    
    - K → Unlocked (overclockable)
        
    - KF → Unlocked, no integrated graphics
        
    - F → No integrated graphics
        
    - T → Low power
        
    - H / HX → Laptop high performance
        
    - U → Laptop low power
        

---

## 2. Intel Generations Overview

- 10th–11th Gen → Older (Comet Lake / Rocket Lake)
    
- 12th Gen → Alder Lake (big.LITTLE hybrid architecture introduced)
    
- 13th Gen → Raptor Lake (improved performance)
    
- 14th Gen → Raptor Lake Refresh (latest widely used)
    

👉 Intel now transitioning to **Core Ultra (Series 1 / 2)** branding (Meteor Lake and beyond):  
Example: **Intel Core Ultra 7 155H**

- Ultra 5 / 7 / 9 → Tier
    
- 1xx → Generation indicator
    
- Focus: AI acceleration + efficiency
    

---

## 🔴 AMD Ryzen CPUs

## 1. Naming Structure

Example: **AMD Ryzen 7 7800X**

- **Ryzen 7** → Product tier
    
    - Ryzen 3 → Entry-level
        
    - Ryzen 5 → Mid-range
        
    - Ryzen 7 → High-end
        
    - Ryzen 9 → Enthusiast
        
- **7** → Generation (7000 series = Zen 4)
    
- **800** → SKU (higher = better)
    
- **Suffix**:
    
    - X → High performance
        
    - X3D → 3D V-Cache (best for gaming)
        
    - G → Integrated graphics (APU)
        
    - U / HS / HX → Laptop variants
        

---

## 2. Ryzen Generations (Desktop)

- 1000 → Zen (1st gen)
    
- 2000 → Zen+
    
- 3000 → Zen 2
    
- 5000 → Zen 3 (very popular)
    
- 7000 → Zen 4 (current mainstream)
    
- 8000 → APUs (not full desktop upgrade)
    
- 9000 → Zen 5 (latest high-end CPUs)
    

---

## 3. Ryzen Laptop Naming (Important ⚠️)

Example: **Ryzen 7 7840HS**

- 7 → Tier
    
- 7 → Year (2023 generation)
    
- 8 → Architecture (Zen 4)
    
- 40 → SKU
    
- HS → Power/performance class
    

👉 AMD laptop naming is more complex than desktop.

---

# ⚖️ Intel vs AMD (Key Differences)

## Architecture

- Intel → Hybrid cores (Performance + Efficiency cores)
    
- AMD → High-performance uniform cores (plus cache advantage in X3D)
    

## Strengths

- Intel:
    
    - Better for mixed workloads
        
    - Strong single-core performance
        
- AMD:
    
    - Better efficiency
        
    - Gaming advantage with X3D chips
        
    - Often better value

# 📅 How to Identify CPU Release Year (Intel & AMD)

Understanding the **approximate release year** of a CPU helps you quickly judge:

- Performance expectations
    
- Efficiency improvements
    
- Platform compatibility (DDR4 vs DDR5, etc.)
    

---

# 🔵 Intel CPUs – How to Tell the Year

## 🧠 Rule of Thumb

👉 Look at the **generation number (first 2 digits after the dash)**

### Example:

- i9-12900H → **12th Gen**
    

---

## 📆 Intel Generations → Year Mapping

|Generation|Codename|Approx Year|
|---|---|---|
|10th Gen|Comet Lake|2020|
|11th Gen|Rocket Lake|2021|
|12th Gen|Alder Lake|2021–2022|
|13th Gen|Raptor Lake|2022–2023|
|14th Gen|Raptor Lake R|2023–2024|

---

## 🧠 Your CPU Example

**i9-12900H**

- 12th Gen → Alder Lake  
    👉 Released around **2022**
    

---

## 🆕 Intel Core Ultra (New Naming)

Example: Core Ultra 7 155H

- **1xx → Series 1**  
    👉 Around **2024**
    

Future:

- Series 2 → ~2025
    
- Series 3 → ~2026
    

---

## ⚠️ Important Notes (Intel)

- Laptop CPUs may release slightly earlier/later than desktop
    
- Same generation can span **1–2 years**
    

---

# 🔴 AMD Ryzen – How to Tell the Year

## 🧠 Desktop CPUs (Easy)

👉 Look at the **first digit of the 4-digit number**

### Example:

- Ryzen 7 7800X → “7” → 7000 series
    

---

## 📆 AMD Desktop Mapping

|Series|Architecture|Year|
|---|---|---|
|1000|Zen|2017|
|2000|Zen+|2018|
|3000|Zen 2|2019|
|5000|Zen 3|2020|
|7000|Zen 4|2022|
|9000|Zen 5|2024–2025|

---

## ⚠️ Missing 4000 / 6000?

- Mostly OEM / laptop-focused
    
- Not standard desktop launches
    

---

## 💻 AMD Laptop CPUs (More Accurate Method)

Example: Ryzen 7 7840HS

- **7 (first digit)** → Year indicator
    
    - 6 → 2022
        
    - 7 → 2023
        
    - 8 → 2024
        

---

### 🔍 Breakdown:

Ryzen 7 7840HS

- 7 → Tier
    
- **7 → Year (2023)**
    
- 8 → Architecture (Zen 4)
    
- 40 → SKU
    

👉 AMD laptops encode **year directly** (unlike Intel)

# ⚖️ Intel vs AMD (Year Detection)

|Feature|Intel|AMD|
|---|---|---|
|Desktop CPUs|Generation-based|Series-based|
|Laptop CPUs|Harder to tell exact year|Encoded in model number|
|Accuracy|Approximate|More precise (laptops)|

## Intel

- First 2 digits after dash = generation
    
- Match generation → year
    

👉 Example:

- 12xxx → ~2022
    
- 13xxx → ~2023

👉 Example:

- 7000 → ~2022

👉 Example:

- x6xx → 2022
    
- x7xx → 2023
    
- x8xx → 2024
